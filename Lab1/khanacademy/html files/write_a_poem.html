The Evening Lantern

The sun begins its slow descent, A brush of gold on velvet blue, The day’s long hours are finally spent, Leaving the world to start anew.
The shadows stretch across the grass, Like quiet giants waking deep, While silver clouds in silence pass, To watch the weary fall asleep.
There is a peace the evening brings, A steady breath, a softened light, Before the moon its lantern swings, To guide us through the gentle night.
